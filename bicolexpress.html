Ingredients

  • 2 packs of sweet chilli pepper (pang-sigang) or Korean Chili
  • 1 pack of raw "alamang" or shrimp fries
  • 1 pack dried anchovies
  • 1 lb pork belly, chopped into desired size or 1 in. x 1/2 in. size)
  • 1 or 2 pork bouillon
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • i medium size onion, chopped roughly
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 5 - 10 pieces labuyo, chopped into small pcs, or 2 tbps chili flakes. Can be adjusted to taste, less or more depending on preference.

Cooking Instructions

  • Step 1Cut sweet chili into strips, take out seeds, then set aside.
  • Step 2Transfer alamang into a strainer and put it under running water and rinse very well. Set aside.
  • Step 3Fry the dried anchovies. Set aside.
  • Step 4In a wok, big casserole, or deep frying pan, put oil then sauté garlic and onion. Add pork and pork boullion and cook meat until color is brown.
  • Step 5Add alamang into the casserole and stir occasionally until ingredients are mixed very well. Alamang absorbs oil so add more cooking oil (probably 3-4 tbsp more) if the dish shows signs of being dry. Cook for 10-15 min or until alamang juice is gone and only the pork fat and cooking oil remain. Reduce heat and simmer.
  • Step 6Add the sweet chili, chopped labuyo (or chilli flakes), and mix with the ingredients very well. Don't overcook. As soon as everything is mixed, turn off the heat.
  • Step 7Add the fried anchovies and mix well.
  • Step 8Serve either as a side dish or main dish. Enjoy!
